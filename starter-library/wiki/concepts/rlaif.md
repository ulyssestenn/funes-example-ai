# RLAIF (RL from AI feedback)
A variant of [RLHF](./rlhf.md) in which the preference labels that train the reward/preference model are generated by an **AI model** instead of human annotators. Introduced in [Constitutional AI](./constitutional-ai.md) for harmlessness: a model evaluates which of two responses better follows a written principle, often using [chain-of-thought](./chain-of-thought.md) reasoning to make the judgment more accurate and transparent; those AI [preferences](./preference-learning.md) then drive RL.

RLAIF is a step toward **scalable oversight** — using AI to help supervise AI — cutting the human labeling bottleneck while keeping humans in control via the high-level principles. In Constitutional AI, helpfulness labels stayed human while only harmlessness labels were AI-generated.
